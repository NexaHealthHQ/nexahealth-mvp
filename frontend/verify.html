Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about drug verification

A "Verified" status means the drug matches an approved NAFDAC registration with high confidence. Both the product name and registration number correspond to official records.

If you receive a "Flagged" result, it means this drug has been reported multiple times as suspicious or potentially counterfeit. Do not consume it. Report it to NAFDAC and seek medical advice.

A "Partial Match" occurs when the product name matches a NAFDAC-approved drug but the registration number doesn't match. This could indicate a counterfeit or mislabeled product. Double-check the NAFDAC number on the packaging.

Our verification system has a 97% accuracy rate based on NAFDAC's official records. The match confidence score helps you assess the reliability of each result. Always cross-check with physical packaging details.
